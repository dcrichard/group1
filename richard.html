Progressive Web Apps

Progressive Web Apps beginning.

Imagine waking up in the morning, grabbing your phone, and visiting the website of your local train company. You quickly check the schedule of the train that will get you to work, close your browser, and put the phone back in your pocket. At the end of the day, you visit the site again and check when the next train departs (not even noticing that the elevator you are riding has no cell reception because the train company's site now works even when you are offline). The next day when you visit the website again, your browser asks you if you would like to add a shortcut to it on your home screen, and you happily agree. Later that day when you launch the site from an icon on your home screen, it lets you know that due to some construction work, delays may be possible and asks you if you would like to receive notifications about future changes to your commute. The next morning, as you are waking up, you receive a notification on your phone that your train has a 15-minute delay. You hit the snooze button on your alarm and gain a few more precious moments of sleep.


Trends for PWA are Conversational Interactions allowing the user to use the app without touching the phone. Accessing information offline with the use of a service worker allows any user to use the full app without being online. Multi-Channel Push Messages allows the Website owner to push notification to your phone at any time. Recieving updates on the fly, for train stop times or opening and closing of stores, etc. Touch Gestures keeps the feel of your mobile or tablet currently adding the additional functionality of more accurate and offline capability.


Being this is sucha a new technology the trend for this application is mostly from a developer point of view. I will imagine that this trend will escalate as soon as more and more individuals use this application and more developers begin to program PWA's.

Breakdown of operation

What started as a simple website has slowly acquired new powers until it was juts as capable as any native app on your phone. Instead of trying to send you to the app store, hoping you will install their app, the train company has earned a permanent place on your phone--one step at a time.


This new progressive model replaces the binary installed/ not installed nature of native apps. Progressive web apps build trust with their users and acquire new powers as they are needed.

PWA Pros and Cons

Pros

List of Pros for using Progressive Web Apps

  • Offline Mode
  • App-like way
  • Improved Performance
  • Zero Install
  • Portability
  • No app store submission

Cons

List of Cons for using Progressive Web Apps

  • Not all Browsers are Supported.
  • Not all native device software functionality supported.
  • There is no central download store.
  • Platform limitations.
